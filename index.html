FAQ

Before you book, here's what people usually ask.

Every project gets a fixed, itemized quote after the discovery call — based on scope, not hours. Most sites land between a few hundred to a few thousand dollars depending on complexity, and you'll know the exact number before any work starts. No hourly billing, no surprise invoices.
Most single-page or marketing sites take 2–3 weeks from kickoff to launch. Larger builds with e-commerce, custom CMS structures, or automation pipelines usually run 4–6 weeks. You'll get a specific timeline in your quote, and weekly check-ins so you always know where things stand.
Every project includes two structured revision rounds — one after the Figma design phase, one after the initial build. Beyond that, small tweaks are still welcome; larger scope changes are quoted separately so timelines stay predictable for both of us.
Yes — most people who book don't have a finished brief. The discovery call exists to figure that out together: your goals, your audience, and what the site needs to do for your business. Come with rough ideas or even just a problem you're trying to solve.
You do, fully. Once the project is paid in full, all files, accounts, and platform ownership (Webflow, Wix Studio, or Shopify) transfer to you. There's no lock-in — you're free to manage, edit, or move the site whenever you choose.
Every project includes a 14-day support window after launch to catch anything that needs adjusting. After that, ongoing support and maintenance retainers are available if you'd rather not manage updates yourself.
